Tag Families

Common tag definitions.

Magic School

The story happens in a school where magic shapes class life, friendships, and conflicts.

Found Family

Characters become a chosen support system instead of staying emotionally alone.

Slow Burn

The relationship builds gradually across scenes or chapters instead of becoming immediate.

Comfort Read

The tone stays gentle or emotionally safe even if the story still has tension.

Harbor Mystery

A mystery story with docks, ships, glowing ports, or coastal magic rather than harsher thriller energy.

Rivals to Allies

Characters begin in conflict and slowly move toward trust, teamwork, or care.

Protective Team

Teammates cover for one another, defend each other, or stay loyal under pressure.

High-pressure Week

A short, intense event window drives the pacing, tension, and emotional stakes.

Late-night Confession

A major emotional reveal happens in a quieter after-hours scene.

Epistolary

The story uses letters, notes, journals, or messages as part of how the plot unfolds.

Soft Politics

There is court or power tension, but the tone stays more character-focused than brutal.

Visible Content Notes

Warnings are shown directly on cards and story pages instead of being hidden behind shorthand.

Safety Guide

Content notes should show up early.

General

Good for readers who want the softest entry and fewer intense themes.

Teen

Allows more tension or romance, but still keeps warnings readable before opening the story.

Visible warnings

A story card should state things like storm danger, crash anxiety, or panic spells directly.

Comfort-first browsing

If you want lighter results, pair a comfort tag with an explicit content note instead of guessing.

Use Case Examples

How these tags work in practice.

Theme path example

Include: Magic School, Found Family, Visible Content Notes.

Good for: a safer first read with an easy emotional payoff.

Similar-story example

Include: Rivals to Allies, Protective Team, High-pressure Week.

Good for: readers who know the dynamic they want and want closer match explanations.

Drama example

Include: Epistolary, Slow Burn, Soft Politics.

Good for: emotional intensity without an unreadable wall of metadata.
