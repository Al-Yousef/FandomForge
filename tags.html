Tags

Plain-language tag help for archive browsing.

This page teaches readers what common archive tags mean, how to use them in search, and how to avoid confusing shorthand when they are just getting started.

Start here Tag families Include or exclude

Fast advice

Read tags as promises, not decoration.

A tag usually tells you what kind of story energy, relationship pattern, or warning to expect before you open the work.

Start Here

If you are new to archive tags, remember these three rules.

Read rating and warnings first

They tell you whether the story is even in the right comfort range before you read the rest.

Use a few tags, not every tag

One relationship tag, one setting tag, and one tone tag is usually enough to start.

Exclude what you know you dislike

Filtering out one unwanted element can be easier than naming the perfect story in advance.

Tag Families

Common tags translated into beginner-safe language.

Setting

Magic School

The story takes place in a school where magic or special training shapes daily life.

Relationship

Found Family

Characters become a chosen support system even if they are not related by blood.

Pacing

Slow Burn

The relationship develops gradually over many scenes or chapters instead of becoming obvious right away.

Tone

Comfort Read

The story is meant to feel emotionally safe, soothing, or gentle even when it has tension.

Dynamic

Rivals to Allies

Characters start in competition or conflict and slowly move toward trust, respect, or partnership.

Dynamic

Protective Team

The emotional payoff comes from teammates defending, covering for, or looking out for each other.

Situation

High-pressure Week

The story compresses tension into a short, stressful time frame like a tournament, race week, or deadline.

Scene type

Late-night Confession

A major emotional reveal happens in a quieter late-night scene after the characters stop performing for everyone else.

Format

Epistolary

Part of the story is told through letters, messages, notes, or other in-world documents.

Tone

Soft Politics

There is court or power tension, but the emotional tone stays more intimate than brutal.

Include or Exclude

How to use tags without getting trapped in filter overload.

Example 1

Include: Found Family, Magic School

Exclude: Graphic Violence

This points you toward the Moonwake-style results.

Example 2

Include: Rivals to Allies, Protective Team

Exclude: Major Character Death

This points you toward the Neon-style results.

Example 3

Include: Slow Burn, Comfort Read

Exclude: Intense psychological horror

This points you toward gentler recommendation rows.
